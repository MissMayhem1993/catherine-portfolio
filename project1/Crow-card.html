American Crow

Crows have the same brain-weight-to-body ratio as humans. Studies have shown crows to be self-aware. Young will take time to learn from tolerant parents.

  • Scientific Name: Corvus brachyrhynchos
  • Average Size: 16–21 in, almost half of which is tail and a wingspan is 33–39 in. Mass varies from about 11 to 21 oz
  • Average Lifespan: 7-8 yrs wild, up to 30 yrs in captivity
  • Habitat: wilderness, farmlands, forests, fields, urban areas, parks, near water sources, towns and major cities. The one place you won't find this bird is the tundra habitat
  • Diet: omnivorous, eating insects, seeds, fruits, small animals, carrion, and human food scraps

Crows are social birds, more often seen in groups than alone. These groups sometimes number in the hundreds of thousands. American Crows work together to harass or drive off predators, a behavior known as mobbing.
